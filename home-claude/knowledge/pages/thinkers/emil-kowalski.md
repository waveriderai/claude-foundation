# How Emil Kowalski thinks

One line: taste is a *trainable* instinct, not a gift — build it by exposing yourself to the best
work — and animate only when motion serves the interface; often the best animation is none.

Design engineer known for animation and interface craft (Sonner, Vaul; "Animations on the Web" course).

## Core mental models
- **Taste is a trained instinct.** "The ability to see beyond the obvious and recognize what
  elevates." It "comes down to trying to expose yourself to the best things that humans have done and
  then... bring those things into what you're doing" (he cites the Steve Jobs framing). → taste is
  *buildable* by deliberate exposure, which is the whole premise of the [[design-taste]] skill and of
  [[learn-from-thinkers]]. ([emilkowal.ski/ui/developing-taste](https://emilkowal.ski/ui/developing-taste))
- **Animate with purpose, or not at all.** "The goal is not to animate for animation's sake, it's to
  build great user interfaces." Every animation must answer *why does this animate?*
- **Don't animate frequent / keyboard-initiated actions.** "These actions may be repeated hundreds of
  times a day, an animation would make them feel slow"; "used multiple times a day, this component
  would quickly become irritating." → **"Sometimes the best animation is no animation."** This is the
  precise discipline that separates intentional motion from AI-default motion excess (scattered
  micro-interactions are a documented AI tell — see [[design-taste]]).
- **Novelty over copy-paste effects.** Generic NPM confetti / formulaic generated effects "fail to
  spark joy because we've all seen them 100 times before." Distinctiveness needs a fresh move, not a
  library default.

## How to apply (to escaping AI-default design)
- Treat motion as a budget you spend once: one well-orchestrated moment (a page-load reveal) beats
  effects sprinkled everywhere — and *less* animation reads as *more* intentional.
- Build taste deliberately: keep a swipe-file of the best work; the skill encodes "expose → absorb →
  apply" as a repeatable loop, not a one-off.
- Before adding any animation, ask "why does this animate, and is it used often enough that motion
  would annoy?" — if yes, cut it.

Pairs with [[thinkers/rauno-freiberg]] (restraint + material mastery) and underpins [[design-taste]].
