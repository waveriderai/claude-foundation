# How Rauno Freiberg thinks

One line: quality is a function of patience and focus, not scale — master the real materials
(HTML/CSS/JS), solve the problem with *less*, and let the details emerge from building in high
fidelity, close to the final medium.

Staff Design Engineer at Vercel (ex–Browser Company / Arc). Author of the *Web Interface
Guidelines* — "a non-exhaustive list of details that make a good (web) interface."

## Core mental models
- **Quality = patience + focus, not resources.** "Quality is not a result of scale or resources.
  Quality is a function of patience and focus." Distinctive work comes from disciplined attention,
  not more tooling or more volume. ([spaces.is](https://spaces.is/loversmagazine/interviews/rauno-freiberg))
- **"A solution to a problem, with less."** His definition of good design. "The ingenuity lies in
  solving a problem with what's available, not introducing more, and playing to the strengths and
  limitations of existing materials." → restraint as the core move (this is the [[design-taste]]
  "spend boldness once, cut the rest" principle, stated from the designer's side).
- **Master the real materials.** "The materials you need to master are HTML, CSS, and JavaScript,
  not design or vibe coding tools." Craft lives in the medium itself — which is exactly why his work
  doesn't read as AI: it's material-true and made by hand in code.
- **Details emerge from implementation, not static vectors.** "A lot of the details come to life
  through actual implementation and consideration of nuances that I just don't encounter working
  with static vectors." Prototype in high fidelity, close to the final medium, as early as possible.
  ([ui.land](https://ui.land/interviews/rauno-freiberg)) → don't design in Figma-stills then hand to
  AI; build the real thing and find the detail there.

## How to apply (to escaping AI-default design)
- Treat the browser as the design tool. The "feel" the user wants (lean, precise, alive) is found by
  building and tuning in real code, not by prompting for a mockup.
- When a design feels generic, the fix is usually *subtraction* — remove until only the one
  necessary move remains. "With less" is a design test, not a slogan.
- Track his *Web Interface Guidelines* as a concrete checklist of interface details.

Pairs with [[thinkers/emil-kowalski]] (restraint + trainable taste) and underpins [[design-taste]].
